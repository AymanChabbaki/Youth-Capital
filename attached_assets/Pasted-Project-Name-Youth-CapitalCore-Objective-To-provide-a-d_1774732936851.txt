Project Name: Youth CapitalCore Objective: To provide a digital civic governance simulation platform where youth practice politics, simulate the Moroccan government, and develop leadership skills.Target Audience: Driven youth across Morocco, the Moroccan diaspora, and the broader African/Arab world.Primary Languages: Bilingual (Arabic and English).2. User Roles & PermissionsThe User (Simulation Participant)Can register and apply for specific roles (Minister, MP, Local Council, Diaspora Rep).Can access the Community forum to debate and vote.Can view the calendar for live sessions and events.Can submit support tickets.Can edit their personal profile and track their simulation progress.The Admin (Platform Manager / Game Master)Can approve, reject, or assign roles to applicants from the Form page.Can trigger "Crisis Scenarios" that notify all users.Can moderate the Community discussions (delete posts, ban users).Can publish news on the Press page and schedule Events.Can respond to and close Support tickets.4. Platform Architecture (Page Specifications)Home PageHero Section: Dynamic --navy-dark background with the white logo. Bilingual headline (English/Arabic) explaining the platform's core mission. --gold CTA button linking to the Form page.About Section: A clean breakdown of "What we simulate" and "What you gain" using --ice-blue cards with icons.Statistics Bar: Live counters (e.g., Active Ministries, Bills Passed, Active Members).Footer: Links to rules, how-it-works, privacy policy, and social media.Form Page (Application & Onboarding)Multi-step registration process for better user experience.Step 1: Basic personal details and language preference.Step 2: Role selection dropdowns (Region, Ministry preference, Parliament house).Step 3: Motivation text box or file upload for their application.Confirmation screen with a tracker showing application status (Pending, Approved).Community Page (The Simulation Hub)Secured area accessible only to logged-in users.Sub-forums categorized by structure: Parliament House, Regional Councils, Specific Ministries.Live chat or threaded discussions for real policy debates.Voting poll integration for passing simulated laws or resolutions.Press Page (News & Updates)Public-facing blog or newsfeed.Features "In-Simulation" news (e.g., "Ministry of Health Announces New Policy") and "Real-World" platform updates.Articles structured with title, date (--slate), author, and thumbnail image.Events Page (Live Sessions)Interactive calendar showing upcoming live sessions with political experts and guests.Countdown timers for major simulated events (e.g., "Annual Budget Vote").Links to external video conferencing tools (Zoom/Google Meet) or embedded live streams.Support PageFAQ section covering rules, how-it-works, and technical troubleshooting.Contact form generating a ticket for the Admin dashboard.Live status of the platform (Operational, Under Maintenance).5. Technical & Functional RequirementsCore FunctionalityBilingual Toggle: A permanent site-wide switch (EN/AR) that changes the UI and text direction (RTL for Arabic, LTR for English).Authentication: Secure login using email/password, with optional Google/Apple single sign-on.Notifications: Email and in-app alerts for role assignments, event reminders, and admin-triggered "Crises."Performance & SecurityMobile-first responsive design, ensuring the platform works seamlessly on smartphones.Data encryption for user details, especially considering the collection of geographic and personal data.Scalable database architecture to handle an increasing number of forum posts and concurrent users during live events.
